Cleaning Checklist

Following this order will help you achieve the best results.

1.Accessing the Property
Front Door - Unit Entry
  • Enter through the front door using the door code -- this will be communicated to you separately
  • Key in the code, wait a second, then turn the deadbolt to unlock
  • To lock from outside: press the Schlage button and turn the deadbolt to the left -- no code needed
  • Keep the door locked at all times
Basement / Laundry Access
  • The washer and dryer are located in the basement
  • Go to the side of the house and enter through the side door using the code provided separately
  • The side door auto-locks 5 seconds after entering the code -- no need to lock manually
  • Once inside, go down to the basement using the interior basement door
2.Exterior Check -- On Arrival
  • Check the porch and front steps -- sweep with the broom if needed
  • Check the side of the house -- pick up or dispose of any garbage on the ground
  • Do a slow video walk-through of all rooms upon entry
  • Record and photograph anything damaged or unexpected -- include close-ups of stains or broken items
  • Send videos and photos to WhatsApp right away so we can address any issues promptly
  • Confirm the TV remote is present and place it under the TV
3.Laundry -- Start First
Starting laundry first ensures it is done on time. Record how long each cycle takes so you can switch loads without delays.
Collect
  • Bed sheets and pillowcases
  • Comforter covers (wash only if visibly stained or smelly)
  • Towels and kitchen cloths
  • Shower mats and bar mops used for cleaning
Do not overload the machine. Overloading causes tangling, poor drying, and wasted time rearranging loads. Wash one load at a time.
Suggested Order
  • Load 1: Bed sheets, pillowcases, comforter covers
  • Load 2: Towels, kitchen towels, shower mats, bar mops
Wash Settings
  • No visible stains: short cycle, cold water, 2 rinses, OxiClean
  • Visible stains: regular cycle, hot water, 2 rinses, OxiClean
  • OxiClean is safe on colors. If unavailable, bleach can be used on whites only
Drying and Standards
  • Always dry on the highest heat setting
  • After drying, check for remaining stains
  • If not fully clean, use the spare set from the closet downstairs next to the laundry room
  • All towels in the unit must be stain-free -- stained towels go to the downstairs closet, kept neat and separate
  • Always have clean dry bar mops available in the unit during cleaning
4.Kitchen
Fridge and Freezer -- Do First
  • Remove and dispose of anything left behind -- check both fridge and freezer
  • Wipe inside with diluted lavender solution; dry with a clean dry bar mop
  • Wipe outside doors with bar mop + glass cleaner; dry immediately
Dishes
  • If guests left dirty dishes, take photos before washing
  • Dishwasher is disconnected -- do not use it, but check inside for dishes guests may have left
  • Wash all dirty dishes by hand; dry with a bar mop -- all dishes must be completely dry before storing
  • Check cabinets and drawers for streaks or water marks -- use paper towel + glass cleaner
  • Pots and pans that are hard to clean: use a steel wool scrubber
  • If a pot or pan is too burned to restore, inform us immediately -- we will replace and file a guest claim
  • Unit must be stocked for at least 6 guests -- communicate any shortages right away
Restock Each Cleaning
  • 1 roll of paper towels
  • Dish soap
  • 1 dish sponge
  • 1 clean dish cloth
Dusting Before Wiping
  • Dust top of fridge, top of range hood, cabinet tops, and any surface prone to dust accumulation before wiping
Surfaces -- Wipe Then Dry Immediately
  • Both microwaves (inside and out) -- there are two, check and clean both
  • Small appliances, kitchen sink, backsplash, all countertops, cabinet doors
  • Range hood outside and especially the top
  • Use diluted lavender floor cleaner -- always dry right away with a clean dry bar mop
Stove
  • Clean the stove top thoroughly
  • If scrubbing needed: apply ceramic stovetop cleaner with a paper towel, leave ~10 minutes while working on something else, then scrub, rinse, and dry with bar mop
Oven
  • Check the oven -- if it needs cleaning, communicate immediately so we can coordinate
  • Oven cleaning is an extra chore and will be paid as such
Dining Area and Soft Furnishings
  • Glass dining table: wipe with bar mop + glass cleaner, dry with bar mop
  • Dining chairs and sofa: spot clean stains with damp cloth + cleaning solution; dry as much as possible
  • Carpet: inspect for stains -- spot clean with damp cloth and cleaning solution
Garbage
  • Dispose garbage and recycling in the correct large bins on the side of the house
  • Line both bins: black = garbage, blue = recycling -- never leave without a liner
  • Wipe inside bins with diluted lavender cleaner and dry with bar mop
  • Leave 3 extra black bags and 3 extra blue bags in the bin drawer
  • Place bins upright (not sideways) -- the drawer will not close otherwise
  • Bins have labels -- reposition the same way to avoid hitting the drainage pipe under the sink
  • Be gentle with cabinet doors -- if something is not closing properly, do not force it, ask us
Once the kitchen is done, switch laundry to the dryer and start the second load if needed.
5.Washroom
  • Move all items (bins, soap, shampoo) into the hallway so you can work comfortably
  • Dust surfaces prone to dust before cleaning the floor -- dusting after mopping dirties the floor again
  • Vacuum floor first to remove hair -- hair sticks to a wet bar mop and makes cleaning much harder
  • Vacuum again at the end together with the rest of the unit
Shower
  • Spray inside shower walls, niche, corner panels, shower pan, inside glass door, and crevice under door with diluted bleach (1 part bleach to 10 parts water)
  • Leave bleach 3 to 5 minutes, then rinse thoroughly
  • Dry all shower surfaces completely -- walls, shower pan, and glass door inside and out must all be dry
  • Remove shoes if needed to reach corners
When spraying bleach, avoid getting it on walls or any painted surfaces to prevent damage.
Toilet
  • Pour concentrated bleach inside bowl and scrub with toilet brush
  • Spray outside of bowl with diluted bleach -- including behind, base, hinges, and hard-to-reach corners
  • Leave 2 to 5 minutes, wipe with damp cloth, then dry with clean dry bar mop
Sink and Vanity
  • Clean with diluted bleach mix, dry with bar mop
  • Clean inside drawers and under sink cabinet with diluted lavender cleaner; dry with bar mop
  • Wipe and dry all washroom items (bottles, soap, bins) before placing back
Restock Washroom
  • Hand soap and shampoo
  • 1 hand towel on towel rack
  • Toilet paper in holder and vanity drawer -- amount communicated per booking; if unsure, ask
  • Bathroom bin: line with small bag; leave extra bags in sink cabinet drawer
Hallway Closet
  • 6 large towels
  • 5 hand towels
  • No face towels in the unit
  • Extra towels and bedding: downstairs closet beside the laundry room
Mop washroom floor with diluted lavender floor cleaner.
6.Bedrooms and Living Room
Dusting -- Before Vacuuming
  • Dust all horizontal surfaces gently so dust falls to the floor and gets vacuumed up
  • All furniture tops, nightstands, shelves, coffee table, TV stand
  • Top of fridge and top of range hood
  • Window bottom seals and blinds (dust gently)
TV and Walls
  • Dust the TV gently -- remove fingerprints with a small amount of window cleaner on a paper towel -- do not spray directly on the TV
  • Spot clean wall marks gently -- be careful not to remove paint
  • Spot clean any marks on the baseboards
Beds
  • Remove sheets from dryer -- check for stains or smell before making beds
  • Make all beds using double pillowcases for each pillow
  • Spray pillows and bed coverings with Island Escape Liv Fresh Linen and Room Spray; also spray sofa cushions and pillows
Vacuuming
  • Empty vacuum canister before and after each use -- empty canister only, no need to remove filters
  • Store vacuum in hallway closet together with the mop
  • Vacuum entire unit including washroom floor
  • Vacuum under beds thoroughly
  • Vacuum inside closets and the bottom channel of sliding doors
  • Inspect carpet for stains -- spot clean with damp cloth and cleaning solution
All cleaning supplies should be stored in the bottom corner kitchen cabinet.
Mopping
  • Use diluted lavender floor cleaner at 1:20 ratio -- more concentrated leaves the floor sticky
  • Spray the Rubbermaid microfiber mop head directly -- do not spray the floor
  • For hardwood: mop must be nearly dry; use the back of mop head to dry as you go
  • Mop refill is washable -- include in laundry when dirty or smelly
  • Store mop upside down in hallway closet to dry properly
Wipe and Sanitize All Touched Surfaces
  • TV stand, coffee table, nightstands, top of fridge, wardrobe shelves
  • Kitchen cabinet doors and drawer fronts
  • Wipe with diluted lavender cleaner; dry with bar mop
Staging
  • Check all wardrobes, drawers, and closets -- must be clean and empty of guest belongings
  • Arrange pillows neatly
  • Spray all rooms with lavender air freshener
  • Check floors, lights, and overall presentation
7.Final Steps
  • Store any extra washed, stain-free sheets and towels in the basement closet
  • Take a final video of the cleaned unit and send to WhatsApp right away
  • Communicate if any linens or kitchenware need replacing -- always enough for 6 guests
  • Communicate if anything is worth buying to improve the unit or any worn items need replacing
  • Let us know if any supplies are running low for the next cleaning
  • No question is a wrong question -- always ask if anything is unclear

Thank you for your hard work!
